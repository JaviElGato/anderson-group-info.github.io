Our research/methodology development

 

As and when required for our applied research, the group also undertakes methodology development projects. Below are a few of the methods projects we've taken on over the last few years.


About our methodology development projects

optiCall: Spurred on by the large number of Immunochip-based association projects within the group, we developed a novel genotype calling algorithm to more accurately call the broad frequency spectrum of variants present on the array. optiCall uses both across-sample and across-SNP clustering methodology to more accurately call genotypes at common, low-frequency and rare variants. It is also able to better handle SNPs where the intensity clouds do not fall in the expected positions, as is more often the case with custom-design arrays such as immunochip (as opposed to the off-the-shelf genome-wide arrays). optiCall has now been used in several genetic association studies, including those of ankylosing spondylitis and multiple sclerosis (and of course our own studies in IBD, PBC and PSC). The optiCall software is freely available for download and use. To learn more please visit the optiCall webpage.

There are a number of methodology development projects currently ongoing within the group. These can be broadly separated into two categories, 1) whole-genome sequencing based association methods and 2) inclusion of genomic annotation data into genetic association studies. As part of our PBC immunochip work we devised a method that considers LD when testing for enrichment of functional annotations within genome-wide significant disease loci and we continue to build on this work.

Our research/whole-exome sequencing

 

About whole-exome sequencing

Whole-exome sequencing studies have been a very successful means of identifying rare, highly-penetrant variants underlying Mendelian-like disease such as Miller syndrome or Kabuki syndrome. For extreme forms of common diseases, it has been hypothesised that rare highly-penetrant variants could be underpinning the disease phenotype. Indeed, this hypothesis has recently been shown to be correct, with the identification of a stop variant in IL10R and a non-synonymous variant in XIAP that both cause severe early-onset inflammatory bowel disease.


About our whole-exome sequencing projects

Inflammatory Bowel Disease: We are leading that analysis of the COLORS in IBD project, a project that aims to whole-exome sequence 150 children with severe early onset IBD. Holm Uhlig from the Univeristy of Oxford is the clinical lead and samples are being drawn from across the UK and Europe. All the cases have an age at onset less than six and we are prioritising for sequencing all cases with an age at onset of less than three and who have required surgery. Eva is undertaking the analysis and the project will form a major component of her PhD thesis.

The team/Carl Anderson

 

Carl Anderson

CDF Group Leader

About me

I studied Biomedical science at the University of Sheffield, where I also completed an MSc in Genetic Epidemiology. In 2003 I started my PhD studies at the University of Edinburgh under the supervision of Peter Visscher, and in 2005 moved to the Queensland Institute of Medical Research in Australia to complete my PhD studies with Peter. In 2007 I started a postdoc position at the Wellcome Trust Centre of Human Genetics in Oxford, where I worked on the WTCCC project with Lon Cardon (focussing on the Crohn's disease arm of the project) and an Endometriosis GWAS with Krina Zondervan. In 2009 I moved to Sanger to take up my first group leader position. My group develops and applies statistical and computational tools for screening the human genome for risk factors underlying complex diseases, with a strong focus on immune-mediated phenotypes. For more information about our research, check out our research page.


My research

The group has many large datasets at its disposal. These range from tens of thousands of individuals genotyped using microarrays to thousands of individuals with low-coverage whole genome sequencing data. We also have exome sequencing data available across hundreds of individuals with extremes of common complex immune-mediated diseases. We are increasingly interested in generating and analysis genomic data across these individuals and aim to undertake RNA-seq, CHiP-seq and Dnase-seq studies in the near future. If you are interested in joining the team, just drop me an email including your current CV and a description of your research interests.

Eva Serra

About me

In 2010 I completed a Bachelor degree in Biology (majoring in Cell Biology and Biotechnology) and moved to London to study for an MSc in Human Molecular Genetics at Imperial College London. After graduating, I joined the Metabolic Disease Group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute where I worked as a junior bioinformatician (2011-2012) before starting my PhD. I am interested in Science promotion and public engagement - you can find me explaining DNA to kids at the annual Cambridge Science Festival. My free time is dedicated to music, dancing ballet and of course, cooking!


My research

My PhD project focus on the application of whole-exome sequencing to identify causal variants underlying a classic rare disease (thyroid disease, as part of the UK10K Project) and a rare and extreme form of a common disease (severe early-onset inflammatory bowel disease).

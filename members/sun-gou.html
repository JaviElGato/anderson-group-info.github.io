About me

From withstanding the unending, frost-bitten nightshifts in the outskirts of Seoul to engineering immune cells, modeling gene networks, ascertaining risk factors of autoimmune disorders, organising international conferences, and to being shortlisted as top ten finalists for a Â£100K business prize, I have acquired a diverse skill-set. My primary interest is to use them in translating scientific and technological ideas into changing the society. I lived in France and Canada as a kid, completed my Bachelor degree in Yonsei University with a major in Biotechnology and a minor in Business Administration, as well as a Master degree from Yonsei with a project in the Network Biology Lab. I am currently a PhD student at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute focused in statistical genetics and population and evolutionary genomics of gene regulation. I enjoy moving out of my comfort zone and taking on new challenges, but I also respect discipline as a member of a team for a common purpose.


My research

In Korea, I have conducted projects in structural biology to design drugs against a plant pathogen, in immunobiology to engineer immune cells using cell-permeable transcription factors, and in network biology to identify new gene function of a human pathogen. At the Sanger, I'm studying two immune-mediated liver diseases to identify associated genetic variants such as SNPs and CNVs from various genotyping chips. Candidate variants arising from these efforts will be put forward to conduct functional studies.

About me

I moved to sunny Cambridge in October 2010 to begin my PhD at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. Prior to that, I completed Bachelor degrees in Economics (2008) and Science (2009, majoring in statistics and genetics) at the University of Queensland, and worked as a research assistant at the Queensland Institute of Medical Research (2009-2010). In my spare time, I enjoy playing football, guitar, reading, travelling and photography, though I'm probably more likely to be wasting it playing Football Manager.


Research

My PhD project focuses on the genetics of immune-mediated liver and inflammatory bowel disorders. Using a combination of dense genotyping and whole-genome sequencing approaches, it is hoped that the identification of genetic variants that underlie disease susceptibility along with their biological functions will allow us to develop more complete models of how genetic variation contribute to disease etiology.

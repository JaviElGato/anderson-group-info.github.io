Velislava Petrova

PhD student

About me

Originally Bulgarian, I moved to the UK in 2009 to do an undergraduate course in Virology and Immunology at the University of Bristol. A series of unexpected events brought me to The Sanger in 2011 where I spent a year as a placement student in the team of Prof. Paul Kellam, working on the development of methods for full-genome sequencing of RNA viruses. For this one year I truly fell in love with the institute and the city itself so after completion of my Bachelor’s degree, the choice of a PhD program was almost predetermined. Despite the lack of experience in statistical genetics, my first encounter (of the third kind) with Team Anderson made me hugely interested in the field and gave a different direction to my research interests, shifting them towards the human aspect of host-pathogen interactions. My breaks from science are dedicated to yoga and traveling. My secret passion is gourmet cuisine and I like exploring the ‘science’ of cooking.


My research

I am currently a joint student between the Anderson and Kellam’s teams, trying to bridge the gap not only between the two groups but also in our understanding of the genetic basis of host-pathogen interactions. My PhD project aims to explore the genetic determinants of human immune response to vaccination compared to natural infection and the role of this response in autoimmunity.
